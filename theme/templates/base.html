Green skin pomelo is a pomelo variety that first originated in Thanh Tan commune, Mo Cay Bac district, Ben Tre province. Green-skinned pomelo fruit has a spherical shape, an average weight of 1.2 - 2.5 kg/fruit; peel is green to yellowish green when ripe, easy to peel and fairly thin; pink-red grapefruit cloves, tightly bundled and easy to separate from the septum; sweet and not sour (brix: 9.5–12%); aroma; seedless to fairly seedless (possibly up to 30 seeds/fruit); meat/fruit ratio >55% This pomelo variety has been recognized as a national variety by the Ministry of Agriculture and Rural Development of Vietnam. Green-skinned pomelo has been exported to 50 different markets around the world. Under normal conditions, green grapefruit can be kept longer than 15 days.
